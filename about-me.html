About Me


Current Role

Starting in 2025, I will be on the other side of Mass Ave, as a first-year PhD student in MIT's Political Science Department.

Experience

From 2023 to 2025, I had the privilege of working as a pre-doctorate Quantitative Social Science Researcher at Harvard's Institute for Quantitative Social Science with Gary King. Two years went by in a flash, and I am immensely grateful for the opportunities afforded to me.

I earned my Bachelor's Degree from Harvard College in Government with a secondary in Statistics. In my concentration, I studied on the Data Science track, learning to apply statistical and quantitative methods in political science. In particular, my substantive studies focused on surveys and political behavior.

During undergrad, I was fortunate to have worked under Professors Chase Harrison and Gary King on projects as a research assistant. Additionally, I served as an undergraduate Course Assistant for the introductory methods class Gov50, for Professors Matt Blackwell and Suzanna Linn. In the public sector, I worked as a Data Fellow for Bluebonnet, which provides data science services to campaigns, and worked as an Intern and Analyst at the Government Accountability Office.

In my free time, I am a proud #DogDad, and I love to read and cheer on the Harvard Swimming & Diving Team. Go Crim!
